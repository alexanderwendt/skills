# Requirements Analysis

Use this skill to turn product intent into clear requirements.

## Requirements Categories

- Functional requirements: what the system must do.
- Non-functional requirements: performance, reliability, usability, security, accessibility, compliance.
- Constraints: technology, business, legal, time, operational, data.
- Assumptions: unresolved facts currently treated as true.
- Open questions: blockers or uncertainties requiring clarification.

## Output

```markdown
## Requirements

- REQ-001: <requirement>

## Non-Functional Requirements

- NFR-001: <requirement>

## Constraints

- CON-001: <constraint>

## Assumptions

- ASM-001: <assumption>

## Open Questions

- Q-001: <question>

## Traceability

- REQ-001: Source=<source>, Validation=<acceptance criterion or test idea>
```

Do not invent requirements without marking them as assumptions.
